Guide to Wetland Birds

of South Eastern Australia

Australia's diverse wetlands provide food, water, shelter and breeding habitat for a variety of waterbirds. Compared to birds of other habitats, many iconic wetland species are highly conspicuous given there size, preference for open habitat and reliance on water. However others are more cryptic, preferring to stay concealed amongst vegetation and are not readily identifiable from fleeting glimpses.

This app provides a guide to 63 of the non-passerine species which are found in and around the wetlands of South Australia, Victoria and Tasmania. These include Cranes, Storks, Herons, Crakes, Rails, Hens, Ibis, Spoonbills, Cormorants, Waterfowl and the Shorebirds (or waders) which are commonly found away from the coast.

BirdLife Australia and its members are committed to the conservation of wetlands for birds.
